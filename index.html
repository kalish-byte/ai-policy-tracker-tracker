Coverage Gap Analysis

What the trackers collectively miss — or at least don't cover well.

Municipal & City AI Ordinances Moderate Gap

Cities like NYC, San Jose, and Baltimore have passed local AI rules. The NLC City AI Governance Dashboard is a start, but coverage remains thin. Most trackers still stop at the state line.

Enforcement Actions & Outcomes Moderate Gap

Plenty of trackers list what passed. Akin and Holistic AI track enforcement and litigation, but systematic coverage of penalties assessed and compliance rates remains thin. Still largely a "so what" gap.

AI Procurement Rules Moderate Gap

Government AI procurement standards and requirements are scattered across agency-specific guidance. No single tracker aggregates them comprehensively.

Sector-Specific Trackers Moderate Gap

Manatt's healthcare AI tracker is the standout, but financial services, defense/intel, and employment AI rules remain fragmented. No tracker besides Manatt does a single sector end-to-end across jurisdictions.

Soft Law & Voluntary Commitments Moderate Gap

White House AI commitments, industry pledges, standards body frameworks — the OECD tracks some, and AlgorithmWatch cataloged 160+ ethical guidelines before going dormant in 2024. The full universe of non-binding AI governance remains poorly mapped.

Global South AI Governance Moderate Gap

Africa, Latin America, and Southeast Asia are actively legislating AI. The OECD, CAIDP Index (75+ countries), and Oxford Insights (195 countries) have expanded coverage, but deep analysis of non-Western jurisdictions remains thin.

Litigation & Case Law Moderate Gap

AI-related lawsuits are shaping policy as fast as legislation. Akin and Holistic AI both include litigation, but systematic case law tracking with outcomes remains sparse. A whole parallel universe, still mostly untracked.

Real-Time Regulatory Guidance Minor Gap

Agency guidance, interpretive letters, and FAQ updates move fast and often matter more than statutes. A few trackers cover this, but most focus on enacted law.
