## 3. Categorization Review

### Current Categories
Your draft uses four categories: Government, Think Tank, Law Firm, Industry.

### Missing Category Types to Consider

**Sector-Specific Trackers**
This is the biggest taxonomic gap. You have no category or tag for trackers that focus on a specific industry vertical. The Manatt Health AI Policy Tracker (healthcare) is a prime example. Other sectors with dedicated tracking activity include financial services (FINRA's 2026 Oversight Report covers AI risks), defense (CNAS research, though not a formal tracker), and employment/HR (Littler Mendelson tracks workplace AI legislation across states). A "sector" tag or filter would let users find these.

**Standards & Frameworks Trackers**
None of your 31 entries focus on AI *standards* (NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, IEEE 7000). These are distinct from legislation — they're technical governance frameworks that increasingly have regulatory force (the EU AI Act references harmonized standards). NIST's own AI Standards page and the ICC's AI Governance and Standards paper could fit here.

**Municipal / Subnational Trackers**
Your coverage gap section correctly flags this, but the NLC City AI Governance Dashboard (listed above) actually fills part of this gap. Adding a "subnational" scope tag or filter would surface it.

**Enforcement & Litigation Trackers**
Your gap analysis notes this, and Holistic AI Tracker 2.0 partially addresses it. Akin's tracker already includes litigation, but it's not called out in the categorization. A tag for "includes enforcement/litigation" would help.

**Readiness Indices & Benchmarks**
Oxford Insights' Government AI Readiness Index and CAIDP's Democratic Values Index are neither legislation databases nor policy trackers — they're comparative assessments. A "Benchmark/Index" category or tag would distinguish them from legislative databases.

**Soft Law / Voluntary Frameworks**
AlgorithmWatch's AI Ethics Guidelines Global Inventory and UNESCO's monitoring framework track non-binding governance instruments. Your gap section flags soft law as undertracked, so adding entries that address it (and tagging them) would be valuable.

### Recommended Taxonomy Enhancement

Rather than replacing your four categories, consider adding **filterable tags** for:

1. **Scope subtypes:** Federal, State, Municipal, International, EU-specific
2. **Content type:** Legislation, Standards/Frameworks, Soft Law/Guidelines, Enforcement/Litigation, Readiness Index
3. **Sector focus:** Cross-sector (default), Healthcare, Financial Services, Employment/HR, Defense/National Security
4. **Audience:** Compliance teams, Researchers, Government affairs, General awareness

This would make the quiz recommender and compare features significantly more powerful without disrupting the existing four top-level categories.
